# Product

## Register

product

## Users

ADHD adults aged 25-45 who experience task paralysis — the inability to start tasks despite knowing what needs to be done. They are often frustrated by clinical productivity tools that treat the symptom (disorganization) rather than the cause (inability to initiate). They use this tool when overwhelmed, scattered, or frozen.

## Product Purpose

TaskRoulette breaks task paralysis by gamifying task selection via a prize wheel mechanic. The user brain-dumps everything swirling in their head, an AI structures it into a concrete list, and a physical-feeling prize wheel picks the next task for them. Success = the user feels rewarded for *starting*, not just completing. The wheel removes decision fatigue and the dopamine from the spin motivates action.

## Brand Personality

Electric, warm, momentum. Playful enough to feel fun, focused enough to feel trustworthy. Voice: a supportive friend who happens to be incredibly organized. Never clinical, never preachy, never hustle-culture.

## Anti-references

- Todoist, Things 3, Linear — too clinical and structured, visually cold
- Beige/cream wellness apps — feel passive, not energizing
- Generic SaaS card grids — no personality, feels like a spreadsheet
- Neon gaming aesthetics — too aggressive, anxiety-inducing for ADHD users
- Confetti for everything — only use it where it means something

## Design Principles

1. **Dopamine first** — every interaction should feel like a small win, not a chore
2. **Friction is the enemy** — starting must feel easier than avoiding; never add steps
3. **Delight is not decoration** — animations are functional; they signal reward and progress
4. **One thing at a time** — the wheel picks, the user acts, repeat; never overwhelm
5. **Warmth over efficiency** — this is a feelings tool, not a productivity tool; tone matters more than features

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. prefers-reduced-motion must be respected on ALL animations — provide crossfade or instant fallbacks. Minimum 44x44px touch targets throughout. High contrast text (4.5:1 minimum).
